Classification of Annual Income of the Population

The chart illustrates that the majority of the selected adult male (47.1%) and female (61.1%) population belong to the Low-Income Group (LIG). This reflects their economic status as daily wage workers with limited income sources. A smaller portion falls under the Lower Middle-Income Group (LMIG), while a very small number belong to Upper Middle-Income and High-Income Groups, with only one male in the HIG category.

Evaluation of Nutritional Status of the Population (BMI)

According to WHO's Asian BMI classification, a majority of the population is categorized as overweight or obese. Only a small percentage of individuals fall into the normal or underweight categories, with possible contributing factors including sedentary lifestyles, unvaried staple food consumption, and genetic predisposition.
