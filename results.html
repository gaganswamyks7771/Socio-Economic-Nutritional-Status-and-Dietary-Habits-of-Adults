Study Results

Socio-Economic, Nutritional Status and Dietary Habits of Adults (18-45 Years)

Demographic Profile

A total of 120 adults aged 18 to 45 years participated in the study. The demographic characteristics revealed a majority were between the age group of 26 to 35 years, with a balanced representation of both genders.

Socio-Economic Status

The socio-economic status of the participants was analyzed using income, education, and occupation. A significant portion belonged to the lower middle and upper lower classes, which may influence their dietary habits and nutritional status.

Anthropometric Measurements

  • Average height: 165.2 cm (males), 155.3 cm (females)
  • Average weight: 68.5 kg (males), 61.4 kg (females)
  • BMI distribution:
    • Underweight: 12%
    • Normal: 46%
    • Overweight: 28%
    • Obese: 14%

Nutritional Status

The analysis showed varying levels of nutritional adequacy. Micronutrient deficiencies were observed in a notable proportion, especially iron and Vitamin D deficiencies among females.

Dietary Habits

  • Meal frequency: Most participants consumed 3 meals a day.
  • Skipping breakfast: 38% admitted to skipping breakfast regularly.
  • Consumption of fruits and vegetables: Average of 2 servings/day, below recommended levels.
  • Junk food intake: 60% consumed fast food at least twice a week.

Quality of Life Assessment

Participants with better dietary patterns reported higher quality of life scores. Sedentary lifestyle and poor dietary choices were linked with lower physical and mental health outcomes.

Effect of Dietary Counselling

Post-counselling evaluations showed a 20% improvement in healthy food choices, and 15% of overweight individuals reported weight loss in 3 months after intervention. Follow-up is recommended for sustained outcomes.
